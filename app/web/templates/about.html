About BioMolAMR

Zero-Shot Antimicrobial Resistance Prediction via Protein–Chemical Compatibility

Abstract

Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) poses a critical global health threat. Computational prediction of resistance associations between genes and novel antibiotic drug classes — zero-shot generalization — is essential for proactive surveillance. We audit prior zero-shot AMR methods and uncover a systematic data-leakage problem: gene feature vectors encoded 46-dimensional drug-class membership vectors, directly embedding the labels being predicted and inflating zero-shot MRR by 5.7×.

With this leakage removed, we show that clean pairwise compatibility scoring between ESM-2 protein embeddings and molecular fingerprints (Feature-MLP) achieves ZS-all MRR 0.069 — 3.1× above a random baseline of 0.022 — outperforming graph neural network architectures including our proposed BioMolAMR heterogeneous GAT model. Ablation confirms both ESM-2 gene embeddings and molecular drug fingerprints are jointly necessary; removing either feature modality collapses performance to or below random. These results demonstrate that direct protein–chemical compatibility is sufficient and dominant for zero-shot AMR transfer; graph topology, while biologically motivated, provides no additional benefit on the current CARD knowledge graph.

Methods
Data
  • CARD v3.2.6: 6,397 resistance genes, 46 drug classes
  • 9,053 training gene–drug pairs, 524 ZS test pairs
  • 12 drug classes held out for zero-shot evaluation
  • Drug SMILES sourced from PubChem / ChEMBL
Gene Features
  • ESM-2 (esm2_t12_35M_UR50D) protein language model
  • 480-dim per-sequence embeddings
  • No drug-class membership information
  • 6,397 genes embedded (12 GB GPU, ~45 min)
Drug Features
  • Morgan fingerprint: 2048 bits, radius 2
  • MACCS keys: 167 bits
  • Topological torsion: 1030 bits
  • Combined: 3245-dim binary vector
Evaluation Protocol
  • Primary: ZS-all MRR (rank among all 46 classes)
  • Random baseline: 1/46 ≈ 0.022
  • 5-seed mean ± std reported
  • Precision@20 for case study analysis

Comparison with Prior Work
Prior ZS-within MRR 0.775 was measured on a 5-way within-ZS ranking (random = 1/5 = 0.20) with leaky gene features. Our ZS-all MRR uses all 46 classes as negatives (random = 1/46 ≈ 0.022), is far more rigorous, and is unaffected by drug-class leakage.
