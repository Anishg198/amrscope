Key Finding

Our main scientific contribution

Prior Work Was Inflated 5.7×
Gene features included 46-dim drug-class membership vectors — directly encoding which drug classes each gene resists. Zero-shot evaluation became trivial since the answer was embedded in the features.
CrossContrastAMR: Cross-Attention + Structural Alignment
Gene representations (ESM-2 → MLP → 128-dim) attend over all 46 drug class embeddings via cross-attention. A Tanimoto structural alignment loss forces the drug space to preserve chemical similarity — enabling true zero-shot transfer. ZS-all MRR 0.090 — 4.1× above random (0.022), 30% over the Feature-MLP baseline.
Why Cross-Attention Works for Zero-Shot AMR
The structural alignment loss ensures chemically similar drugs cluster together in embedding space. When a novel drug appears at test time, its fingerprint embedding lands near training drugs with similar structure — genes that attended to those drugs naturally score it correctly. Graph topology (GNNs) adds no additional benefit.

How It Works

Two complementary feature spaces encoded and combined

🧬
Gene Encoder
ESM-2 protein language model generates 480-dim amino acid sequence embeddings per resistance gene — no drug-class information, no leakage.
💊
Drug Encoder
Molecular fingerprints (Morgan 2048 + MACCS 167 + TopoTorsion 1030 = 3245 bits) encode drug chemical structure. Tanimoto similarity measures structural proximity.
⚡
Compatibility Score
Gene and drug embeddings are projected to 128-dim space and scored via dot product. Higher scores predict stronger resistance association.
